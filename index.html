About

Samal, a municipality in Bataan, Philippines, has a rich history that dates back to the pre-colonial era when it was inhabited by indigenous Aeta communities. During Spanish colonization, Samal became one of the early settlements established in Bataan, contributing to the province's agricultural and cultural development. Its fertile lands supported farming, while its coastal location enabled fishing and trade. Over the years, Samal played a role in the country's fight for freedom, with its residents participating in the Philippine Revolution and World War II. Today, Samal preserves its historical heritage while embracing progress and modernity.
